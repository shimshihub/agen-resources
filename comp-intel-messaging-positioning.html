Key Takeaways for Agen for Work

P1 (Governed Access) Is the Most Contested Pillar

14 of 23 competitors claim some form of permissions or access control. But only Zenity, Peta, and Runlayer offer approval workflows. Agen's differentiation must be employee-identity-aware permissions tied to existing IAM - not just agent-level tokens or MCP-level RBAC.

P2 (Total Visibility) Has Strong Incumbents

Noma ($132M funded), CrowdStrike (Falcon platform), and Zenity all deliver deep observability. Agen's P2 differentiation must be behavioral discovery of shadow agent connections - not just audit logs, but proactively finding which employees are connecting which agents to which tools.

P3 (Scale) Is Where Bundling Kills

Microsoft (Azure), CrowdStrike (Falcon), and Workato (iPaaS) can bundle "good enough" reliability and compliance into existing enterprise agreements. Agen's P3 story must lead with cost optimization - something no incumbent offers today - plus multi-geo privacy controls.

7 High Threats, Up from 5

Zenity, Peta, and Runlayer are elevated to High Threat when evaluated through the Work lens. All three are purpose-built for the "govern agent access in the enterprise" use case. Workato is elevated because its 1,400+ connectors solve the "connect agents to tools" problem with bundled governance.

Nobody Owns All Three Pillars

No competitor delivers strong coverage across Governed Access + Total Visibility + Scale simultaneously. Zenity comes closest (Strong P1, Strong P2, Partial P3) but lacks cost optimization and identity-native architecture. Agen's win condition: be the only platform that delivers all three pillars from an identity-first foundation.

The Trust Narrative Is Crowded

"Adopt AI safely," "Build trust into AI," "Adopt with confidence" - Zenity, Fiddler, Eve, and Peta all use trust-unblocking language. Agen must differentiate not on the narrative (trust) but on the mechanism (identity infrastructure). "We don't just secure agents - we connect them to your identity layer so security teams say yes."
