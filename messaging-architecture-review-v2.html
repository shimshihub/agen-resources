Narrative Deep Dives

How each narrative tells its story, and who it speaks to.

The Ghost Agen for SaaS

Your competitors are already accessible inside ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. When a user asks their AI agent to do something your product does - and your product doesn't show up - you're a ghost. You exist, but you're invisible where it matters.

Primary persona: Business Visionaries (CPO, VP Product, Head of Strategy)
Emotional driver: Fear of irrelevance. Competitive urgency.
The Context Gap Agen for Work

AI agents are incredible. But an agent without access to your business data is just a chatbot. The real power unlocks when agents can securely reach into Salesforce, Jira, your internal tools - through governed MCP connections that respect identity, permissions, and context. Without that layer, you're getting 10% of what AI can actually do for your organization.

Data & Context
Agents connected to the right business data through governed MCP channels
Identity & Access
Every connection respects who the user is and what they're allowed to access
Governance & Control
Visibility, enforcement, and audit trails across all agent-to-app connections
Primary persona: Guardians (CISO, VP IT, Compliance) + Business Visionaries
Emotional driver: Aspiration to unlock AI's full potential - combined with the need to do it responsibly.
Productivity Gap Agen for Work

Other companies are already using AI agents connected to their business tools. They're building faster, operating leaner, and shipping more. Every quarter you wait, the gap widens - not because they have better people, but because their people have better tools.

Primary persona: Business Visionaries (CEO, COO, VP Operations)
Emotional driver: Competitive speed. Fear of falling behind.
Enterprise Readiness SaaS Work

You have a basic MCP or agent connection. It works in demos. But enterprise customers need more: per-tenant controls, delegated admin, compliance audit trails, behavioral analytics. The gap between "it works" and "it's production-ready" is where deals stall.

Primary persona: Technical Builders (SaaS) + Guardians (Work)
Emotional driver: Trust and credibility. Closing enterprise deals.

Why "The Context Gap" works for Work

It leads with AI's promise, not its threat. The story is aspirational - "unlock the full potential of AI for your organization" - while naturally pulling in governance, identity, and control as the how. Security, visibility, and enforcement become features of the solution rather than the problem statement itself. This avoids positioning Agen as another security observability vendor and instead frames it as the infrastructure layer that makes AI actually useful inside the enterprise.

Three Refinements to Sharpen Structure 1

1 Lead with context, not security

The Work narrative should open with "AI is amazing, here's how to unlock its full potential" - not "your agents are out of control." Governance, identity, and enforcement are the how, not the what. This positions Agen as infrastructure for AI-powered work, not a security product.

2 Map personas explicitly to each narrative

Right now the structure says "matched to ICP and use case" but doesn't show which ICP matches which narrative. Making that explicit prevents the sales team from using the wrong narrative with the wrong buyer. See the mapping table above.

3 Decide where "Cross-sell from CIAM" lives

Motion 1 (cross-sell to 300 existing Frontegg CIAM customers) is your fastest path to revenue but doesn't cleanly fit under either product's narrative structure. It leverages the existing relationship, not a fear-based narrative. It may deserve its own lane as a GTM motion that cuts across both products - or explicit callouts within each product's one-pager.
