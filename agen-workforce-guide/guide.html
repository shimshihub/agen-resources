1 What Agen Workforce is

Agen is a unified platform - one product, one admin surface. The workforce use case is the path for organizations that want their employees to use AI agents (Claude, ChatGPT, custom copilots) safely against company data and internal tools.

The problem it solves: employees are already connecting LLMs to company systems - through ChatGPT plugins, random MCPs they found on GitHub, personal API keys. IT has no visibility and no standard. Agen gives every employee a governed, role-scoped path to connect their agents to company systems, without IT becoming the bottleneck.

Mental model Agen sits between your identity provider (Okta, Entra, Google Workspace, Ping) and the tools employees want to reach with AI (HubSpot, Google Calendar, GitHub, Gmail, internal APIs). It generates a per-employee MCP that carries that employee's identity and permissions. Every agent request flows through Agen, which authorizes, masks, and audits in real time.

2 Day 1: Your empty tenant

When you first land in the Agen workforce console, you'll see your development tenant with empty widgets. This is deliberate - nothing is wired up yet.

The dashboard is read-only until data flows through. The seven widgets tell you what Agen tracks:

  • Using connectors - which employees have any Agen-provisioned connector active
  • Policies enforcement - allow/block decisions over time
  • Top active tools - which apps employees' agents are hitting most
  • AI actions - count of agent-initiated operations this month
  • Top active connectors - popular connectors across your workforce
  • Pending requests - employee access requests waiting on IT approval
  • Recent active users - who's using Agen right now

3 Connect your identity provider (Okta SSO)

Before any employee can use Agen, you plug your existing IdP in. The demo uses Okta via SAML 2.0. The wizard walks you through the Okta side without you needing to know SAML.

In Okta Admin: Applications → Create App Integration → pick SAML 2.0.

Step 2: Map attribute statements

Okta and Agen need to agree on how user data (first name, last name, group memberships) travels in the SAML assertion. The wizard pre-fills the mapping.

Step 3: Assign users to the Agen app

Decide who in your organization can use Agen. The simplest choice is to assign the "Everyone" group (all employees), then refine later with finer-grained Okta groups per team.

Phased rollout tip You don't have to open the floodgates. Assign Agen to a pilot group (e.g. agen-pilot) in Okta, and only members of that group will be able to log in to Agen. When you're ready to expand, add more Okta groups - no Agen config change needed.

4 Verify on the Okta side

Once the SAML app exists in Okta, you can see Agen's view of the integration from the Okta admin console too.

5 Configure the connector library

Now the fun part. Agen's connector library is where you decide which internal tools your employees' agents can reach. Each connector can be configured with either Agen-managed credentials or bring-your-own.

The two credential modes matter for different buyers:

Frontegg credentials

Agen handles the OAuth app registration and token management for you. Fastest path - pick a tool, flip it on, done. Best for pilots and smaller deployments where you don't want to manage yet-another-OAuth-app.

Bring your own

You paste in your own HubSpot (or whatever) OAuth app credentials. Your legal/security team gets full provenance - traffic flows through your OAuth app, not Agen's. Best for regulated industries and enterprise security teams who want to own every credential.

6 End-user experience: Claude

Here's where the value shows up for employees. Once you've connected Agen, every Agen-provisioned connector appears directly in Claude's Organization settings → Connectors list. Employees don't set up MCPs themselves - Agen provisions them identity-scoped.

Each connector that appears in Claude is scoped to the employee logged in. So when two employees open Claude, they see the same list of connector names, but each one is wired to their own tokens and permissions - not a shared service account.

7 End-user experience: ChatGPT

Same story in ChatGPT. The employee types a natural-language request, mentions the connector with @-syntax, and Agen handles the authorization flow.

The OAuth flow is the key moment. Agen doesn't pre-connect every tool for every employee - that would over-grant permissions. Instead, when a user first asks for something a tool can't do without their consent, Agen kicks off just-in-time OAuth.

Note The success page mentions "hubspot account" in the copy even though the demo flow was for Google Calendar. In production, this copy should be dynamic per connector. Worth flagging to engineering.

8 User lifecycle management

Agen doesn't replace your IdP - it defers to it. When you need to add or remove an employee, you do it in Okta (or Entra, or whichever IdP you connected), and the change propagates to Agen automatically.

Why this matters Offboarding is automatic. When HR deactivates an employee in Okta, they lose Agen access at the IdP layer - you don't have to remember to revoke them from a second system. No orphaned accounts.

9 Recap: what you just built

If you followed this end to end, your organization now has:

  1. A governed Agen tenant where your employees sign in with their existing Okta identity.
  2. Connectors wired up for the tools that matter (HubSpot, Google Calendar, GitHub, etc.), with per-employee OAuth handled just-in-time.
  3. Those connectors visible in Claude and ChatGPT, identity-scoped so each employee only sees and acts on their own data.
  4. User lifecycle tied to your IdP - no separate account list to maintain.

What you didn't have to do: build MCPs from scratch, register OAuth apps per employee, write policy code, or configure identity provisioning. All of that is the platform.
