Three Refinements to Sharpen Structure 1

1 Rename "Productivity Gap" - Split it into two narratives

The real emotional hook for Guardians (CISO, VP IT) isn't "we're less productive" - it's "employees are already connecting agents to our data and we have zero visibility." Consider "The Blind Spot" for Guardians - the fear of ungoverned, invisible agent access. Keep "Productivity Gap" as a secondary narrative for business leaders buying Work, where the competitive speed argument resonates more.

2 Map personas explicitly to each narrative

Right now the structure says "matched to ICP and use case" but doesn't show which ICP matches which narrative. Making that explicit prevents the sales team from using the wrong narrative with the wrong buyer. See the mapping table below.

3 Decide where "Cross-sell from CIAM" lives

Motion 1 (cross-sell to 300 existing Frontegg CIAM customers) is your fastest path to revenue but doesn't cleanly fit under either product's narrative structure. It leverages the existing relationship, not a fear-based narrative. It may deserve its own lane as a GTM motion that cuts across both products - or explicit callouts within each product's one-pager.

Key asymmetry to preserve

Enterprise Readiness is secondary for SaaS (the Ghost opens the door, enterprise features close the deal) but primary for Work (governance IS the entry point, productivity gap justifies the budget). This asymmetry is correct - don't flatten it into a shared narrative.
