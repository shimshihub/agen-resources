Target Personas

CISO / Head of Security

Primary Buyer
The person currently blocking agent adoption. Responsible for the org's security posture. Under pressure from business teams to enable agents but lacks the controls to say "yes."
Pain Points
  • Shadow AI: employees using agents without monitoring
  • No visibility into which agents access which tools
  • Compliance risk: no audit trails (SOC2, GDPR)
  • Fear of being responsible when a breach happens
  • Pressure from business teams to "just allow it"
What They Need to Hear
  • "Say yes without compromising your security posture"
  • "Every agent action tied to an employee identity"
  • "Human-in-the-loop for sensitive operations"
  • "Compliance-ready logs, not just monitoring"
Discovery Questions
  1. Are employees using AI agents to access business tools today? Do you have visibility into it?
  2. What's your current policy - blanket block or case-by-case?
  3. What compliance frameworks do agent interactions need to satisfy?
  4. If you could see and control every agent-to-tool connection, would you approve broader access?
Outreach Snippet

"Your employees are already using agents - you just can't see it. Agen gives you the identity-aware controls and audit trails to say 'yes' to agent adoption without compromising your security standards."
