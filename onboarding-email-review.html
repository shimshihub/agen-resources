ImportantBoth Products

Welcome email lands in Gmail Promotions tab

Tested: Email 1 goes to Gmail Promotions instead of Primary inbox. Likely culprits: embedded image, multiple links, HTML-heavy design. This is the first touchpoint - if they don't see it, the rest of the sequence fails.

Try removing the header image as a first test. If needed, simplify to a more text-forward, personal-feeling email with fewer links. Transactional-style welcome emails perform better for inbox placement.
View inbox placement improvement steps

Test in this order (easiest first):

  • Step 1: Remove the 600x120 header image. This is the single biggest signal to Gmail that an email is promotional.
  • Step 2: Reduce the number of links. Currently Email 1 has 13 links. Try cutting to 3-4 max (Quick Start Guide, one feature link, Slack).
  • Step 3: Remove social media icons (Facebook, LinkedIn, X, Slack, Email). These are classic promotional signals.
  • Step 4: Reduce HTML complexity. The simpler the email looks (closer to a personal email), the more likely it lands in Primary.

Nuclear option (if nothing else works):

Send Email 1 as a plain-text-style HTML email - minimal formatting, no images, 1-2 links. Something that looks like a personal welcome from the team. This almost always lands in Primary. You can keep the branded, image-heavy design for Emails 2 and 3 since by then the sender reputation is established.
