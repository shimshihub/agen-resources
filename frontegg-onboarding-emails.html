Key Observations

Cadence pattern: All 2025 branches use a day-of-week cadence (Mon - Thu - Tue - Mon), delivering emails over ~2 weeks.

InfoSec CTR is 0% across all 5 emails - either low volume, deliverability issues, or content not resonating with this persona.

Founders #2 performs best (8.33% CTR) - worth analyzing what that email does differently.

Developer #1 is the strongest opener (4.63% CTR) but engagement drops sharply in later emails (1.14%, 1.2%).

Legacy flow has 240 contacts waiting in the 22-day delay queue and 64 in the 7-day queue. These are active contacts getting 2023 content.

Structural Notes

Shared pattern: All branches start with Email #1 - 10 min delay - Slack notification - then a parallel branch splits off (1-day or 5-day delay for internal tracking).

Legacy uses fixed day delays (22 days, 7 days) tied to trial lifecycle. Persona branches use day-of-week scheduling instead.

Developer has 4 emails while InfoSec and Founders have 5 - Developer may need a 5th email added for consistency.

No suppression logic visible - contacts who convert or become customers may continue receiving nurture emails.

Improvement Recommendations Suggestions

High Fix InfoSec 0% CTR Across All 5 Emails

Every InfoSec email shows 0% click rate. This could indicate: (a) very low volume making stats unreliable, (b) deliverability problems, (c) content that doesn't resonate with security personas, or (d) no trackable links in the emails. Investigate root cause before investing in new content for this branch. If volume is the issue, consider broadening the "InfoSec" job title group matching.

High Replace Legacy 2023 Fallback Emails

The "None met" branch uses 3 emails from September 2023. With 240 contacts in the 22-day delay queue and 64 in the 7-day queue, these are actively reaching contacts with 2.5-year-old content referencing outdated product features and messaging. Either update these templates or create a new generic persona branch with current content.

High Add Suppression / Conversion Logic

No visible goal-based suppression in any branch. Contacts who convert (book a demo, start a paid plan, become a customer) may continue receiving onboarding nurture emails. Add if/then branches or goal criteria that exit contacts from the nurture when they hit conversion events.

Medium Investigate Engagement Drop-off in Developer Branch

Developer #1 has 4.63% CTR but #3 drops to 1.14% and #4 to 1.2%. This is a common nurture fatigue pattern. Consider: shorter sequence, more compelling mid-funnel content, or adding conditional branches based on engagement (e.g., if they clicked email #1, send different content for #2).

Medium Add Missing Persona Branches

Current branching only covers Developer, InfoSec, and Founders. Product/PM roles, VP/Director of Engineering, DevOps/Platform, and general business roles all fall into the legacy "None met" path with 2023 content. Consider adding at least a "Product Leader" and "General Tech" branch.

Medium Add 5th Email to Developer Branch

InfoSec and Founders both have 5 emails while Developer only has 4. For consistency and to maximize the nurture window, consider adding a 5th Developer email. Look at what Founders #2 (8.33% CTR) and #4 (4.76% CTR) do well and adapt that approach.

Introducing Agen into Frontegg Onboarding Agen

Every Frontegg CIAM customer is a potential Agen cross-sell (Motion 1). The existing nurture sequences run for ~2 weeks with 4-5 emails per persona. Adding a single Agen touchpoint within the existing flow - rather than after it - ensures maximum visibility while the contact is still actively engaging.

Recommended Insert Agen Email as #3 or #4 in Each Branch

The existing sequences already have 4-5 emails per persona. The sweet spot for an Agen teaser is email #3 (Thursday send) - the contact has received 2 Frontegg-specific emails and is still in the engagement window. Replace or insert an Agen-flavored email that bridges from their CIAM setup to the AI-readiness narrative. Not a hard sell - an educational bridge.

Developer Version "Your CIAM is Already AI-Agent Ready"

Subject: Your identity layer is the foundation for AI agents

Angle: "You're setting up Frontegg for authentication - did you know this same identity infrastructure can secure AI agent access to your product? Meet Agen." Link to dev docs or a quick explainer. Speak to the Technical Builder pain point of MCP complexity.

InfoSec Version "AI Agents Are Coming - Your IAM Is Ready"

Subject: How to govern AI agent access before it becomes a problem

Angle: "AI agents are already accessing enterprise tools. With Frontegg's identity layer, you're positioned to govern this with Agen." Speak to the Guardian's fear of uncontrolled agent access and compliance gaps. This might also help improve the 0% CTR by introducing a more compelling topic.

Founders Version "The AI Visibility Gap - Don't Be a Ghost"

Subject: Is your product visible to AI agents?

Angle: "Products invisible to AI agents become invisible to users. Your Frontegg CIAM is the fastest path to making your product AI-agent accessible." Use the Ghost narrative. Link to AmIAGhost.com or an Agen landing page. Founders #2 already gets 8.33% CTR - this topic could perform even better.
