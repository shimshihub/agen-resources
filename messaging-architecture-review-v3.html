Narrative Deep Dives

How each narrative tells a connectivity story.

The Ghost Agen for SaaS Primary

Your competitors are already accessible inside ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. When a user asks their AI agent to do something your product does - and your product doesn't show up - you're a ghost. You exist, but you're invisible where it matters.

Connectivity angle: Your product has no agent connectivity. Agents can't find you, can't reach you, can't use you. You're disconnected from the agentic economy.
Primary persona: Business Visionaries (CPO, VP Product, Head of Strategy)
Emotional driver: Fear of irrelevance. Competitive urgency.
The Context Gap Agen for Work Primary

AI agents are incredible. But an agent without access to your business data is just a chatbot. The real power unlocks when agents can securely connect to Salesforce, Jira, and your internal tools - through governed channels that respect identity, permissions, and context. Without that connectivity layer, you're getting 10% of what AI can actually do for your organization.

Connectivity angle: Your agents have no app connectivity. They can't reach your data, can't act on your behalf, can't access the tools your teams use every day. The gap isn't intelligence - it's connection.
Primary persona: Guardians (CISO, VP IT) + Business Visionaries
Emotional driver: Aspiration to unlock AI's full potential - responsibly.
Productivity Gap Agen for Work

Other companies are already using AI agents connected to their business tools. They're building faster, operating leaner, and shipping more. Every quarter you wait, the gap widens - not because they have better people, but because their people have better-connected tools.

Connectivity angle: Your competitors' employees have better agent-to-app connectivity. Their AI works with their data. Yours doesn't - yet.
Primary persona: Business Visionaries (CEO, COO, VP Operations)
Emotional driver: Competitive speed. Fear of falling behind.
Enterprise Readiness SaaS Work

You have a basic MCP or agent connection. It works in demos. But enterprise customers need more: per-tenant controls, delegated admin, compliance audit trails, behavioral analytics. The gap between "it works" and "it's production-ready" is where deals stall.

Connectivity angle: Your connectivity exists but isn't production-grade. It needs identity, governance, and scale to serve enterprise requirements.
Primary persona: Technical Builders (SaaS) + Guardians (Work)
Emotional driver: Trust and credibility. Closing enterprise deals.

The mirror that ties it together

The Ghost and The Context Gap are mirror images of the same connectivity problem. One is about being unreachable (SaaS). The other is about being unable to reach (Work). Both are solved by the same infrastructure layer - Agen.

Pressure Testing "Connectivity"

? Does "connectivity" feel too generic?

It could describe Zapier or Workato. The differentiator is that Agen's connectivity is identity-aware and MCP-native. It's not just piping data between apps - it's making agent-to-app connections that respect who the user is, what they're allowed to do, and what the agent is permitted to access. The word "connectivity" is the category. The modifiers "identity-aware" and "governed" are the differentiation.

? Does it work for the cross-sell motion?

Yes - and it makes the cross-sell pitch cleaner than before. For existing CIAM customers: "You already manage identity for your users. Now extend that same identity layer to govern how AI agents connect to your product (SaaS) or how your employees connect agents to their tools (Work)." Identity is the bridge. Connectivity is the new capability.

? Does it create a platform story?

Yes. Agen is the connectivity layer. Work is one side (agents connecting to apps). SaaS is the other side (apps becoming connectable to agents). Customers on both sides benefit from the same infrastructure. This is harder to tell with security-led or context-led positioning, where the two products feel like separate tools.
