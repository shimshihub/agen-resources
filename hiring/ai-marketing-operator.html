AI Marketing Operator

An entry-level launchpad to run our AI-native marketing engine. Mountain View, hybrid 3 days/week.

Part 1: The Strategic Brief

Why this role, now

Dan ran our marketing team and doubled as our PMM. With him moving on, the default move is to backfill with another senior PMM - and I think that’s the wrong shape for where we actually are.

Here’s the real tell: our CEO, Sagi, is personally spending a lot of his time, honestly too much, building decks, one-pagers, and other assets himself. When the CEO is hand-making collateral, the bottleneck isn’t strategy or messaging. It’s production.

Over the past year we’ve built a serious marketing engine - automated processes running on Claude Code, Lovable (GTM Spark), and more - that already generates a large share of what we ship.

What it produces today (partly), and what we want much more of:

  • One-pagers and personalized slides
  • Case studies and blog posts
  • Social posts
  • Videos - marketing/brand and personalized
  • Competitor comparisons for specific deals
  • Product-feature focus pieces
  • Sales enablement and marketing enablement material
  • Email nurture flows
  • A hand in the onboarding and in-product experience
  • And more

These asks will increasingly arrive as a queue. We’re standing up an intake - Slack or a dedicated internal app - where sales and the team request materials. This person is the one who clears that queue fast, without dropping quality. They need to be a delivering machine.

What’s missing isn’t strategy. Our positioning, messaging, and value prop are already established, and the exec team pivots them when the market calls for it. What’s missing is the human-in-the-loop who takes the engine and their own craft and turns us into a producing machine - someone who runs the engine end to end, finishes its output to a publishable bar, keeps it lean, and extends it. The win: leadership stops hand-making assets, and we stop relitigating a message that’s already set. That person isn’t a PMM. It’s an AI-native operator who loves marketing - the single go-to for marketing and sales, whatever asset anyone needs.

The economics back it up. A junior AI-native operator costs meaningfully less than a senior PMM, and since the engine already exists, the leverage curve favors the operator profile. The right hire is closer to a hands-on producer-operator than a marketing director.

Profile of the ideal hire

AI fluency is the one non-negotiable. Beyond that we’re flexible on background, with a clear preference for someone who comes from marketing. Likely 1-3 years in, but we care more about evidence than years.

  • AI-proficient by default. Lives in AI tools (Claude, ChatGPT) day to day and knows how to prompt them well to get great output. Real prompting experience is a strong plus. This is the foundation of the role.
  • Marketing background preferred. Someone who already understands content and marketing will ramp fastest. We’re open to strong people from adjacent paths, but that’s the lean.
  • Exceptionally organized, high throughput. Can run many different tasks and a steady request queue at once without letting anything slip. This is a delivering-machine role.
  • Obsessive about detail. Our quality bar is high. They’re the last set of eyes, and the reason output looks handcrafted rather than AI-generated.
  • A strong editor, not a writer. They don’t need to generate ideas or write from scratch - the engine drafts and the exec team owns the message. They take that draft and edit it to a polished, on-message, excellent finish.
  • Technical when needed. Understands our product well enough to make accurate content, and comfortable working across AI and no-code tools. Not a coder.

Red flags: wants to own messaging strategy or be the “ideas person”; strong on big-picture thinking but loose on details and follow-through; needs a defined process and a clear brief before they can start; treats AI as an occasional tool rather than how they work.

A day in the role

  • Work the request queue (Slack today, a dedicated intake soon): a demo deck, a closing one-pager, a champion video, a competitor comparison for a live deal. Skim the automated intel briefing for anything post-worthy.
  • For each request, use our internal tools and the AI tools we work with to create new assets or polish ones we already have, get sign-off from the right stakeholder, and release them fast.
  • Extend the engine: a new workflow for a recurring request, a new skill for an asset type we keep making by hand.
  • Flag patterns to leadership (“four requests for the same one-pager this week, let’s standardize it”).

What this role is NOT

Not a content writer (the engine drafts, they finish), not a strategist (positioning and ICP are set by leadership), not a brand manager (the brand exists, they keep output true to it), and not “the AI person” in a corner (they are marketing’s operating layer - the engine is how marketing happens here).

Part 2: Job Description (post-ready)

AI Marketing Operator

Frontegg | Agen.co | Mountain View, CA (hybrid, 3 days/week in office) | Reports to GTM Ops & AI Systems | Full-time

About Agen.co. Agen.co is the enterprise context access layer for AI agents - we help organizations securely expose internal systems, SaaS apps, and data to the agents and copilots their employees are already building, through an identity-aware control layer that governs access and gives security teams real oversight. We’re early, fast-moving, and unusually AI-native: most of what marketing ships comes off an engine of automations, skills, and AI workflows. We’re looking for the person to run that engine, finish its output, and extend it.

The role. You’ll be the go-to person for marketing and sales whenever they need an asset - the human-in-the-loop for our marketing engine. Most of our content, research, and outbound is AI-generated through a stack of internal skills and AI tools. You operate that engine: take a request, create or polish the asset, get it approved by the right stakeholder, and ship it fast - then keep extending the engine as new patterns emerge. This isn’t a traditional product marketing role, and you don’t need a marketing degree. You need a real love for AI tools, an obsessive eye for detail, and the operator instinct that turns a 70% AI draft into something we’re proud to send. This is an entry-level role and a rare launchpad - you’ll operate at the center of an AI-native GTM team and build a portfolio most people won’t touch for years.

What you’ll do. - Take a request for a set of assets and turn it around fast - use our internal tools and the AI tools we work with to create new assets or polish ones we already have. - Cover a wide range of formats: one-pagers, personalized slides, case studies, blog and social posts, marketing and personalized videos, competitor comparisons, email nurture flows, and sales/marketing enablement. Make every one feel handcrafted, not AI-generated. - Get sign-off from the right stakeholder, then release fast. - Own the request queue (Slack today, a dedicated intake soon) and keep the backlog moving. You’re the delivery engine. - Improve the engine: when a request recurs, turn it into a repeatable workflow or skill so it’s faster next time.

What we’re looking for. - Strong AI fluency - you live in tools like Claude and ChatGPT by choice and know how to prompt them well to get great output. This is the foundation. - A marketing background is preferred. Someone who already gets content and marketing will ramp fastest, though we’re flexible for the right person. - Seriously organized and high throughput. You can run many tasks and a steady request queue at once without letting anything slip. This is a delivering-machine role. - An obsessive eye for detail. You’re the last set of eyes, and the reason our assets look handcrafted rather than AI-generated. - A strong editor. You don’t need to come up with ideas or write from scratch - the engine drafts and the exec team owns the message. You take that draft and edit it to a polished, on-message, high-quality finish. - Technical when it counts: enough product understanding to make accurate content, and comfortable across AI and no-code tools (running a skill, wiring up a simple workflow). Not a coder. - Comfort with ambiguity - we give you the system, the messaging, and the context; you figure out the rest.

Nice-to-haves. B2B SaaS exposure (security, identity, or developer tools); comfort on camera or with video editing.

What we’re not looking for. This isn’t a senior PMM role (you won’t own positioning strategy), it isn’t a pure content-writer role (the engine drafts, you finish), and it isn’t a quiet, process-driven environment (the work changes weekly).

How to apply. Send a short note about the most interesting thing you’ve built with AI, with a link or screenshot if you have one. We care more about what you’ve shipped than your resume format. Apply at [link to be added] or DM Ori Shimon on LinkedIn.
