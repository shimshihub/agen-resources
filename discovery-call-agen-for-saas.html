Discovery Goal

Understand where they are on their AI/MCP journey, what's blocking them from going to production, and whether Agen for SaaS solves a real problem for them. By the end you should know if there's a fit - and what to show in the demo.

Mandatory - always ask
Optional - ask if relevant
I

Current State

Understand where they are today with AI and MCP
  • M
    "Tell us about your AI journey so far."Must Ask Opens the conversation wide. Let them talk - you'll learn if they're an Observer, Experimenter, or AI-Native.
  • +
    "What pushed you to build an MCP in the first place?" Reveals if the initiative is customer-driven (strong signal) or internal experimentation.
  • +
    "How long did it take from idea to something usable?" Gauges how much they've invested and how painful the build was.
  • +
    "Is it seeing any adoption? Customer-driven or experimentation?" Separates real traction from side projects. Customer-driven = higher urgency.
Transition: "Interesting - so you've [summarize their state]. Let me ask about what's keeping it from being production-ready..."
II

Readiness Gap

Identify what's blocking them from going live / scaling
  • M
    "What's stopping you from being listed as a connector today?"Must Ask Gets directly at the blockers. Could be security, stability, compliance, or just not having the resources. This is where you find the pain.
  • +
    "What needs to be in place before you feel comfortable opening up your APIs to GenAI more broadly?" Expands from MCP to broader AI accessibility. Often reveals enterprise requirements (auth, compliance, observability).
Transition: "So it sounds like [blocker] is the main thing. Let me dig into a specific scenario..."
III

Forced Autonomy

Anchor the conversation to a real workflow where agents need to act independently
  • +
    "What's one workflow where you can't rely on a human in the loop and an external agent would need to act?" Grounds the conversation in a real use case. Everything that follows should reference this workflow.
  • M
    "If that agent hallucinated or misfired in that workflow, what's the blast radius?"Must Ask Makes the risk concrete. If the blast radius is high, the need for guardrails is urgent - that's Agen's sweet spot.
Transition: "OK so the stakes are real. Let me ask about how you'd control that..."
IV

Control & Authority

Test their thinking on permissions, identity, and governance (light POV testing)
  • M
    "Once an agent can take action there, how are you thinking about controlling what it can act on and who it acts as?"Must Ask Tests whether they've thought about identity-aware access control. If they haven't - that's the gap Agen fills.
  • +
    "For that workflow, are you more concerned with what users allow agents to do, or who the user actually is?" Separates the authorization problem from the identity problem. Both matter - Agen handles both.
Transition: "That makes sense. Now thinking about the platforms your customers are using..."
V

Platform & Data Risk

Understand which AI platforms matter and what data concerns exist
  • +
    "Which GenAI platforms are customers asking for in that workflow?" Maps the landscape - ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, custom agents. Reveals how fragmented their integration challenge is.
  • M
    "How are you thinking about customer data exposure when those platforms are involved?"Must Ask Data exposure is often the CISO blocker. If they don't have an answer, Agen's data governance layer becomes critical.
Transition: "Last question area - if something goes wrong..."
VI

Observability & Accountability

Reveal gaps in tracing, logging, and incident response for agent actions
  • M
    "If an AI agent did something unexpected in that workflow today, how would you trace what happened?"Must Ask Most teams have no answer here. This is the "aha" moment - they realize they have zero observability into agent actions.
  • +
    "Zooming out - what seems harder, building the MCP or controlling it?" Great closing question. If they say "controlling it" - perfect Agen fit. If they say "building it" - Agen still helps with enterprise-grade MCP generation.

Closing the Discovery Call

Summarize what you heard, then propose the right next step based on what you learned:

Strong Fit - Book Demo

They have an MCP or are building one, have real pain around security/control/observability, and there's customer demand. Propose a tailored demo: "Let me show you exactly how Agen solves [their specific blocker]."

Early Stage - Educate First

They're interested but haven't started building. Share the "Ghost" narrative, send them resources, and propose a follow-up in 2-3 weeks after they've explored further.

No Fit - Graceful Exit

They're not a B2B SaaS, have no API, or AI isn't on their radar. Thank them, leave the door open, and add them to nurture.

Always confirm: "Based on what you shared, I think a demo would be valuable. I'd focus on [specific area]. Who else from your team should join? Can we lock in [specific date/time]?"

Pro Tips

70/30 Rule - They should talk 70% of the time. Your job is to ask great questions and listen.
Reference their workflow - Once they give you a specific use case in Act III, keep referring back to it in every subsequent question.
Don't pitch yet - Discovery is for understanding, not selling. Save the product details for the demo.
Mirror their language - If they say "connector" instead of "MCP", use "connector". Match their vocabulary.
Use silence - After they answer, wait 2-3 seconds. They'll often add the most valuable insight in the pause.
Async discovery works too - If scheduling is hard, these questions can be sent over email or Slack. The structure still applies.

Red Flags - Stop & Reassess

  • No API or developer platform - they can't expose anything to agents
  • Pure internal use case only - redirect to Agen for Work
  • "We'll build it ourselves" with no timeline pressure
  • No customer demand or competitive pressure for AI access
  • Can't identify a single workflow where agents would act
  • Security/compliance is not on their radar at all
